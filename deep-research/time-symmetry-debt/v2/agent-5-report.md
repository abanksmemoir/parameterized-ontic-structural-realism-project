# Agent 5 Report: The Gravitational Absorber Condition

## Question
Has anyone formulated an absorber condition for gravitational radiation, analogous to the Wheeler-Feynman absorber condition for electromagnetic radiation?

## Executive Summary

**Yes** -- several attempts have been made, with the Hoyle-Narlikar theory (1964-1974) representing the most complete and explicit formulation of a gravitational absorber condition. However, the gravitational case faces unique structural obstacles absent in the electromagnetic case, and no gravitational absorber theory has achieved the same level of acceptance or completeness as the Wheeler-Feynman theory for electromagnetism. The literature reveals a rich but unfinished program, with important contributions from Rosen (1979), Penrose (1979), Blanchet and Damour (1993), and more recently Duda (2025). A critical physical obstacle -- the near-total transparency of the universe to gravitational radiation -- may make a literal gravitational absorber condition impossible to satisfy, forcing the gravitational arrow of time into a fundamentally different explanatory framework than the electromagnetic one.

---

## 1. Background: The Electromagnetic Absorber Condition

The Wheeler-Feynman absorber theory (1945, 1949) established that classical electrodynamics admits both retarded and advanced solutions to Maxwell's equations. The observed dominance of retarded radiation is not a consequence of the dynamics but of a cosmological boundary condition: if the universe is a "perfect absorber" -- meaning all radiation emitted is eventually absorbed -- then the combined response of all future absorbers produces exactly the radiative reaction force, and the advanced components cancel. The absorber condition states:

> The universe is opaque in the future: all electromagnetic radiation emitted is eventually absorbed.

Key references:
- Wheeler, J.A. and Feynman, R.P., "Interaction with the Absorber as the Mechanism of Radiation," *Reviews of Modern Physics* 17, 157 (1945).
- Wheeler, J.A. and Feynman, R.P., "Classical Electrodynamics in Terms of Direct Interparticle Action," *Reviews of Modern Physics* 21, 425 (1949).

Hogarth (1962) showed that the absorber condition is cosmology-dependent: it is satisfied in a steady-state universe but not in certain expanding Friedmann models with diminishing future absorber density. Cramer (1983) proposed that a boundary condition at the T=0 singularity could substitute for a future perfect absorber, producing the observed dominance of retarded radiation via reflection of advanced waves at the initial singularity.

Key references:
- Hogarth, J.E., "Cosmological Considerations of the Absorber Theory of Radiation," *Proc. Roy. Soc. A* 267, 365 (1962).
- Cramer, J.G., "The Arrow of Electromagnetic Time and the Generalized Absorber Theory," *Foundations of Physics* 13, 887 (1983).
- Partridge, R.B., "Absorber Theory of Radiation and the Future of the Universe," *Nature* 244, 263 (1973). [Experimental test finding no evidence for advanced radiation.]

## 2. The Hoyle-Narlikar Theory: Explicit Gravitational Absorber Formulation

### 2.1 The Theory

The most complete attempt to formulate a gravitational absorber condition is the Hoyle-Narlikar (HN) theory of gravity, developed in a series of papers beginning in 1964. Einstein himself recognized the potential: when Feynman presented the Wheeler-Feynman absorber theory at Princeton, Einstein stated he was trying to achieve the same thing for gravity.

Hoyle and Narlikar constructed a direct-particle-interaction theory of gravity, modeled on the Wheeler-Feynman approach. Their key papers include:

- Hoyle, F. and Narlikar, J.V., "A New Theory of Gravitation," *Proc. Roy. Soc. A* 282, 191 (1964).
- Hoyle, F. and Narlikar, J.V., "On the Gravitational Influence of Direct Particle Fields," *Proc. Roy. Soc. A* 282, 184 (1964).
- Hoyle, F. and Narlikar, J.V., *Action at a Distance in Physics and Cosmology* (W.H. Freeman, 1974). [Comprehensive monograph.]
- Narlikar, J.V., "Action at a Distance and Cosmology: A Historical Perspective," *Annual Review of Astronomy and Astrophysics* 41, 169 (2003). [Review.]

### 2.2 Structure of the Theory

The HN theory uses a Fokker-type action principle for direct interparticle gravitational interaction. The key structural elements are:

1. **No independent gravitational field**: Gravity is not mediated by a field with independent degrees of freedom but arises from direct particle-particle interactions along the light cone.

2. **Half-retarded plus half-advanced propagation**: The mass field m(a)(X) at a point X due to particle a is defined via a Green's function that is the time-symmetric combination: one-half retarded plus one-half advanced. This is the direct gravitational analog of the Wheeler-Feynman electromagnetic prescription.

3. **Machian inertia**: The gravitational constant G is not fundamental but is determined by the mean density of matter in the universe. Inertial mass arises from the gravitational interaction with all other matter -- a realization of Mach's principle.

4. **Recovery of Einstein's equations**: In the smooth-fluid (continuum) limit, the HN theory reduces to standard general relativity with the Einstein field equations.

5. **Cosmological absorber condition**: The theory requires that the universe function as a perfect gravitational absorber, so that the combined advanced response of all future matter produces the correct gravitational radiation reaction and cancels the advanced components, leaving only retarded gravitational waves.

### 2.3 Hawking's Critique

In 1965, Stephen Hawking showed that the HN theory is incompatible with an expanding universe. The technical issue: integrating the advanced gravitational field contributions from all future matter to infinite future time produces a divergent integral in expanding cosmologies.

- Hawking, S.W., "On the Hoyle-Narlikar Theory of Gravitation," *Proc. Roy. Soc. A* 286, 313 (1965).

This was considered a fatal objection for decades. However, it has been noted that the discovery of the accelerating expansion of the universe (1998) may resolve the divergence, because the cosmic event horizon in a dark-energy-dominated universe provides a finite cutoff. The event horizon acts as a boundary that limits the integration to a finite future domain, potentially making the advanced mass component finite and restoring the viability of the HN absorber mechanism.

### 2.4 Status

The HN theory remains the only fully developed gravitational absorber theory in the literature. It was embedded in the quasi-steady-state cosmology, which is now observationally disfavored (it does not fit WMAP/Planck CMB data). However, the mathematical structure of the direct-particle-interaction approach and its gravitational absorber condition remain formally valid as a theoretical framework, regardless of the specific cosmological model.

## 3. Rosen's Gravitational Absorber Analysis (1979)

Nathan Rosen published a provocative paper extending the Wheeler-Feynman absorber analysis to gravitational radiation:

- Rosen, N., "Does Gravitational Radiation Exist?" *General Relativity and Gravitation* 10, 351 (1979).

Rosen showed that near a gravitational source, the field can be decomposed into a time-symmetric combination (one-half advanced plus one-half retarded). Under the assumption that this symmetry persists globally -- i.e., that the gravitational absorber condition is satisfied -- he concluded that a physical system does not lose energy as gravitational radiation.

This conclusion was subsequently falsified by:
1. The Hulse-Taylor binary pulsar observations (1979 onward), which showed orbital energy loss precisely matching the general relativistic prediction for retarded gravitational wave emission.
2. Direct gravitational wave detections by LIGO (2015 onward).

The significance of Rosen's paper is not its conclusion but its methodology: it demonstrates explicitly that the Wheeler-Feynman absorber logic can be applied to gravitational radiation, and that the *outcome* depends critically on whether the universe satisfies a gravitational absorber condition. The observed energy loss from binary systems proves that the universe does *not* function as a perfect symmetric gravitational absorber -- the retarded solution dominates.

## 4. Asymptotic Structure: Bondi-Sachs, Outgoing Radiation Conditions, and Null Infinity

### 4.1 The Outgoing Radiation Condition

A parallel approach to the gravitational arrow of radiation came from the study of asymptotic structure. The foundational work is:

- Bondi, H., van der Burg, M.G.J., and Metzner, A.W.K., "Gravitational Waves in General Relativity. VII. Waves from Axi-symmetric Isolated System," *Proc. Roy. Soc. A* 269, 21 (1962).
- Sachs, R.K., "Gravitational Waves in General Relativity. VI. The Outgoing Radiation Condition," *Proc. Roy. Soc. A* 264, 309 (1961).
- Trautman, A., "Radiation and Boundary Conditions in the Theory of Gravitation," *Bull. Acad. Pol. Sci.* (1958); reprinted arXiv:1604.03145.

Sachs formulated an outgoing radiation condition for gravitational waves based on the asymptotic behavior of the Riemann tensor along outgoing null geodesics. The condition -- analogous to the Sommerfeld radiation condition in electromagnetism -- requires that the curvature falls off in a specific algebraic pattern (the "peeling" property) at future null infinity (scri+, denoted I+).

### 4.2 The Asymmetry Between Scri+ and Scri-

Penrose's conformal compactification framework provides a geometric picture: asymptotically flat spacetimes have both a future null infinity I+ and a past null infinity I-. Retarded radiation propagates to I+; advanced radiation would arrive from I-. The standard outgoing radiation condition imposes that there is no incoming radiation from I- -- this is essentially the gravitational analog of selecting the retarded propagator.

However, as noted in the literature, the Bondi-Sachs formalism was "not really set up in a way that might eliminate incoming gravitational radiation." The retarded coordinate system used is not naturally suited for formulating a condition about the absence of advanced waves. This represents an important technical gap.

### 4.3 BMS Group and Memory Effects

The discovery by Bondi, Metzner, and Sachs that the asymptotic symmetry group at null infinity is infinite-dimensional (the BMS group, rather than the expected Poincare group) has deep implications. The supertranslation symmetries and the associated gravitational memory effect (Christodoulou 1991) are inherently tied to the retarded structure of gravitational radiation. The memory effect -- a permanent displacement of test particles after the passage of a gravitational wave -- is computed as an integral over retarded time of the news function at I+. This formalism is built on the assumption of retarded radiation; the advanced analog would involve I- and has received essentially no attention.

## 5. Gravitational Radiation Reaction and Time-Symmetric Decompositions

### 5.1 DeWitt-Brehme and Tail Terms

The gravitational analog of the Abraham-Lorentz radiation reaction force was first derived by:

- DeWitt, B.S. and Brehme, R.W., "Radiation Damping in a Gravitational Field," *Annals of Physics* 9, 220 (1960).

Their approach is a covariant generalization of Dirac's 1938 method for the radiating electron. A crucial finding: in curved spacetime, the electromagnetic (and gravitational) Green's function does not vanish inside the light cone, producing "tail" terms. This means gravitational radiation in curved spacetime propagates not only on the null cone but also within it -- a fundamental departure from flat-spacetime physics that complicates any absorber analysis.

### 5.2 MiSaTaQuWa Equation and Detweiler-Whiting Decomposition

The modern gravitational self-force program builds on:

- Mino, Y., Sasaki, M., and Tanaka, T., "Gravitational Radiation Reaction to a Particle Motion," *Phys. Rev. D* 55, 3457 (1997).
- Quinn, T.C. and Wald, R.M., "Axiomatic Approach to Electromagnetic and Gravitational Radiation Reaction of Particles in Curved Spacetime," *Phys. Rev. D* 56, 3381 (1997).
- Detweiler, S. and Whiting, B.F., "Self-force via a Green's Function Decomposition," *Phys. Rev. D* 67, 024025 (2003).

The Detweiler-Whiting decomposition is directly relevant: they decompose the retarded Green's function into a "singular" part (constructed from the symmetric Green's function (G_ret + G_adv)/2 plus a specific homogeneous solution) and a "regular" part. The regular part -- which determines the self-force -- is a smooth solution of the homogeneous wave equation.

The adiabatic approximation to the gravitational self-force uses the half-retarded minus half-advanced field, (G_ret - G_adv)/2, which captures the dissipative (radiation-reaction) part of the force. This is precisely the time-antisymmetric component that Dirac identified in 1938. The conservative part requires the full Detweiler-Whiting regular field.

This decomposition echoes the Wheeler-Feynman structure: the radiation reaction arises from the antisymmetric (retarded minus advanced) part of the field, while the symmetric part contributes to inertia/self-energy.

### 5.3 Blanchet-Damour: Time-Asymmetric Structure of Gravitational Radiation

A key paper:

- Blanchet, L. and Damour, T., "Time-Asymmetric Structure of Gravitational Radiation," *Phys. Rev. D* 47, 4392 (1993).

Blanchet and Damour study the "antisymmetric" (retarded minus advanced) component of the linearized gravitational field generated by an isolated system. They show that radiation reaction effects in the dynamics of an isolated system arise specifically from the use of retarded potentials -- i.e., from time-asymmetric boundary conditions imposed at past null infinity. The antisymmetric component is post-Newtonian small, arising from odd powers of v/c, and is responsible for all dissipative effects including energy loss to gravitational waves.

This work demonstrates that the time-asymmetry of gravitational radiation is not a property of the Einstein equations themselves but is injected through the boundary conditions (choice of retarded propagator). The equations are time-symmetric; the asymmetry is structural.

## 6. Penrose's Weyl Curvature Hypothesis as a Gravitational Boundary Condition

### 6.1 The Hypothesis

Penrose proposed in 1979 that the gravitational arrow of time has a geometric origin:

- Penrose, R., "Singularities and Time-Asymmetry," in *General Relativity: An Einstein Centenary Survey* (Cambridge University Press, 1979).
- Penrose, R., *The Emperor's New Mind* (Oxford, 1989), Chapter 7.
- Penrose, R., *Cycles of Time* (Bodley Head, 2010).

The Weyl curvature hypothesis states: **the Weyl curvature tensor vanishes (or is very small) at the initial cosmological singularity (Big Bang) but is unconstrained at final singularities (black holes, Big Crunch).**

### 6.2 Connection to the Gravitational Arrow of Radiation

The Weyl tensor encodes the free gravitational degrees of freedom -- tidal forces and gravitational radiation -- as distinct from the Ricci tensor, which is determined by the local matter content via Einstein's equations. Vanishing Weyl curvature at the Big Bang means:

1. **No gravitational radiation at the initial singularity**: The early universe is conformally flat -- gravitationally smooth and featureless.
2. **Low gravitational entropy**: The initial state has extremely low gravitational entropy, providing the "past hypothesis" for gravitation.
3. **Asymmetric boundary conditions**: The past boundary (Big Bang) is constrained; the future boundary (singularities inside black holes) is not. This is an explicitly time-asymmetric cosmological boundary condition on the gravitational field.

### 6.3 Does It Function as an Absorber Condition?

The Weyl curvature hypothesis is **not** formulated as an absorber condition in the Wheeler-Feynman sense. It does not posit that future matter absorbs all gravitational radiation. Instead, it imposes a *past* boundary condition: the gravitational field starts in a special (low Weyl curvature) state. The observed dominance of retarded gravitational radiation would then be a consequence of this initial condition -- propagating gravitational degrees of freedom forward from a smooth initial state.

This is conceptually different from the absorber mechanism:
- **Wheeler-Feynman**: The arrow of radiation arises from the *future* boundary (sufficient absorbers in the future).
- **Weyl curvature hypothesis**: The arrow arises from the *past* boundary (smooth initial gravitational state).

Both produce the same phenomenological result (retarded radiation dominates), but the explanatory mechanism is inverted. This inversion may be physically necessary for gravity, given the fundamental obstacle discussed in Section 7.

## 7. The Fundamental Obstacle: The Universe Is Transparent to Gravitational Radiation

### 7.1 The Problem

The Wheeler-Feynman absorber condition for electromagnetism works because the universe is opaque to electromagnetic radiation: photons are absorbed after traveling finite distances through charged matter. The universe is electromagnetically opaque at many wavelengths.

Gravity faces the opposite situation. The universe is almost entirely transparent to gravitational radiation:

- The graviton-matter interaction cross section is approximately 4pi^2 * hbar^2 / c^3 ~ 10^{-65} cm^2 (comparable to the Planck area).
- A detector with the mass of Jupiter, at 100% efficiency, orbiting a neutron star would detect approximately one graviton every 10 years.
- The Sun is transparent to its own gravitons.
- The universe is transparent to gravitational waves all the way back to the Planck epoch.

This has been analyzed by:
- Dyson, F., "Is a Graviton Detectable?" (2012 Poincare Prize Lecture); also in *Int. J. Mod. Phys. A* 28, 1330041 (2013).
- Rothman, T. and Boughn, S., "Can Gravitons Be Detected?" *Foundations of Physics* 36, 1801 (2006).

The gravitational absorption cross section is so small that gravitational radiation at any wavelength propagates essentially unabsorbed through the entire observable universe across all of cosmic history.

### 7.2 Implications for a Gravitational Absorber Condition

This transparency creates a seemingly fatal obstacle for any literal gravitational absorber condition. The Wheeler-Feynman mechanism requires that the universe be a "perfect absorber" -- that all emitted radiation is eventually absorbed. For electromagnetism, this is plausible. For gravity, it appears to be flatly impossible: gravitational waves propagate to cosmological distances and beyond without significant absorption.

Even in the Hoyle-Narlikar framework, the "absorber" role is played not by literal absorption of gravitons but by the gravitational influence of all future matter on the mass field. The mechanism is more subtle than photon absorption -- it involves the back-reaction of distant matter through the advanced Green's function. But the extreme weakness of gravitational coupling means this back-reaction is extraordinarily small per particle, requiring the contribution of all matter in the universe to produce a finite effect.

### 7.3 Black Holes as Local Gravitational Absorbers

Black holes are the one known class of objects that function as perfect gravitational absorbers: their event horizons absorb all incoming gravitational radiation with no reflection (in the classical limit). Some authors have suggested that black holes could play the role of gravitational absorbers in a Wheeler-Feynman-type theory.

However, black holes absorb radiation that falls *into* them; they do not provide the future-directed absorption of *outgoing* radiation that the Wheeler-Feynman mechanism requires. A gravitational wave propagating outward from a binary merger encounters the cosmological vacuum, not a screen of black holes. Black holes may function as local absorbers but cannot satisfy a cosmological absorber condition.

## 8. The Cosmological Arrow and Retarded Gravitational Radiation

### 8.1 Gold-Wheeler Hypothesis

A parallel explanatory framework connects the arrow of radiation directly to cosmological expansion:

- Yurova, A., Yurov, A., and Yurov, V., "The Cosmological Arrow of Time and the Retarded Potentials," *Universe* 9(1), 30 (2023).

These authors argue that the cosmological arrow of time (expansion) is the cause of the radiative arrow (retarded potentials), confirming the Gold-Wheeler hypothesis that stars radiate rather than absorb light *because* the universe is expanding. The same mechanism would apply to gravitational radiation.

### 8.2 Cosmological Event Horizon and Future Boundary

In a universe with accelerating expansion (Lambda-CDM), the cosmic event horizon acts as a causal boundary: gravitational waves emitted today will eventually redshift to zero frequency and "pile up" at the horizon. This has several consequences:

1. It provides a finite future boundary for any absorber-type integral, potentially resolving Hawking's divergence objection to Hoyle-Narlikar theory.
2. It effectively prevents any future absorber from receiving radiation emitted in the present epoch, because the radiation never reaches it.
3. It functions as a "sink" rather than an "absorber" -- radiation is lost to the horizon rather than being absorbed by matter.

### 8.3 Green's Functions in FRW Spacetimes

The retarded Green's function for gravitational waves in Friedmann-Robertson-Walker spacetimes has been calculated:

- Chu, Y.-Z., "Green's Functions for Gravitational Waves in FRW Spacetimes," *Phys. Rev. D* 48, 4688 (1993).

A crucial result: in all FRW spacetimes with non-vanishing Ricci scalar, the Green's function violates Huygens' principle -- gravitational waves develop "tails" propagating inside the light cone. This means gravitational radiation in our actual universe does not propagate cleanly on the null cone, complicating any sharp distinction between retarded and advanced propagation.

## 9. Recent Work: Duda (2025) on Experimental Tests

The most recent contribution:

- Duda, J., "Experimental Estimation of Asymmetry of Radiation for Wheeler-Feynman Theory for Gravitational Waves," arXiv:2512.20692 (2025, revised 2026).

Duda proposes that the standard assumption of purely retarded gravitational waves (ratio 1:0 retarded:advanced) should be tested experimentally. He introduces an "asymmetry parameter" alpha representing the advanced-to-retarded ratio and argues LIGO observations could constrain it, since strain measurements are invariant under time-reversal symmetry.

He points to several observational puzzles as potential evidence for a small advanced-wave component:
- Neutron star mergers lacking expected electromagnetic counterparts.
- Gravitational wave events (e.g., GW190521, GW190403) with luminosity distances implying ages exceeding the universe's current age.
- Pulsar Timing Array signals requiring more supermassive black hole binaries than predicted.

While this paper is speculative, it represents the first proposal to use gravitational wave observations to empirically test the retarded-radiation assumption.

## 10. Summary: What Exists, What Is Missing, What Remains Open

### What Exists

| Topic | Key Contributors | Status |
|-------|-----------------|--------|
| Gravitational absorber condition (direct formulation) | Hoyle & Narlikar (1964-1974) | Complete theory, but tied to disfavored cosmology; Hawking's divergence objection partially resolved by cosmic acceleration |
| Time-symmetric gravitational field decomposition | Rosen (1979) | Demonstrated the absorber logic applies to gravity; falsified by binary pulsar and LIGO observations as a physical claim |
| Outgoing radiation condition for gravity | Sachs (1961), Trautman (1958), Bondi et al. (1962) | Well-established but imposed by hand, not derived from absorber-type arguments |
| Time-asymmetric structure of gravitational radiation | Blanchet & Damour (1993) | Rigorous demonstration that time asymmetry enters through boundary conditions, not dynamics |
| Past boundary condition (Weyl curvature hypothesis) | Penrose (1979) | Proposed but unproven; provides alternative to future-absorber mechanism |
| Gravitational self-force and time-symmetric decomposition | DeWitt-Brehme (1960), MiSaTaQuWa (1997), Detweiler-Whiting (2003) | Well-developed; radiation reaction extracted from (ret - adv)/2 component |
| Cosmological arrow and retarded potentials | Gold, Wheeler, Yurova et al. (2023) | Connects expansion to radiative arrow; applies to gravity in principle |
| Experimental test proposals for advanced gravitational waves | Duda (2025) | Speculative but novel; no empirical results yet |

### What Is Missing

1. **A gravitational absorber condition that works in standard cosmology**: The Hoyle-Narlikar formulation requires either a steady-state universe or relies on the cosmic event horizon in Lambda-CDM, neither of which has been rigorously shown to satisfy the absorber condition for gravity.

2. **A resolution of the transparency problem**: The universe's near-total transparency to gravitational radiation means no known mechanism can provide the "future absorption" required by a literal Wheeler-Feynman absorber condition for gravity. This is the single most important structural difference between the electromagnetic and gravitational cases.

3. **A derivation (rather than assumption) of the outgoing radiation condition**: In both the Bondi-Sachs asymptotic analysis and the post-Newtonian framework, the choice of retarded propagator is imposed, not derived. No one has produced a gravitational analog of the Wheeler-Feynman derivation showing that the absorber response *produces* the retarded propagator.

4. **Connection between the Weyl curvature hypothesis and the radiative arrow**: Penrose's hypothesis imposes a past boundary condition on the gravitational field. Whether this mathematically implies the dominance of retarded gravitational radiation has not been rigorously demonstrated.

5. **Attention from the gravitational wave community**: The LIGO/Virgo/KAGRA community has not treated the absence of advanced gravitational waves as a foundational issue. All data analysis assumes retarded waveform templates. There is no systematic search for advanced-wave signatures.

### What Remains Open

The central open question is: **Why are all observed gravitational waves retarded?**

Three candidate answers exist in the literature:

1. **Future absorber mechanism** (Hoyle-Narlikar): The universe absorbs all gravitational radiation, producing the retarded solution. This faces the transparency objection.

2. **Past boundary condition** (Penrose): The Weyl curvature vanishes at the Big Bang, imposing smooth initial data from which gravitational degrees of freedom propagate forward. This avoids the transparency problem but shifts the explanatory burden to the initial condition.

3. **Cosmological expansion** (Gold-Wheeler-Yurova et al.): The expanding universe breaks the time symmetry, favoring retarded over advanced propagation. This is compatible with either of the above but has not been made rigorous for gravity specifically.

None of these has been elevated to the status of a proven theorem or an experimentally confirmed mechanism. The gravitational case is fundamentally harder than the electromagnetic case because (a) gravity cannot be screened, (b) the universe is transparent to gravitational waves, and (c) the nonlinearity of general relativity makes the superposition arguments central to Wheeler-Feynman theory only approximate.

## 12. Conclusion

The gravitational absorber condition has been formulated -- primarily by Hoyle and Narlikar in the 1960s-70s -- but it has never achieved the explanatory power of its electromagnetic counterpart. The fundamental obstacle is physical rather than mathematical: the universe is a near-perfect absorber of electromagnetic radiation but a near-perfect *transmitter* of gravitational radiation. This asymmetry between the two sectors means that the Wheeler-Feynman mechanism, which relies on complete future absorption, cannot straightforwardly operate for gravity.

The selection of the retarded propagator in the gravitational sector currently rests on a combination of:
- The outgoing radiation condition (Sachs, Trautman), imposed by hand.
- The Penrose Weyl curvature hypothesis, proposing a past boundary condition rather than a future absorber.
- General cosmological arguments linking the radiative arrow to the expansion of the universe.

This represents a genuine conceptual gap: the electromagnetic sector has a structural account of why retarded radiation dominates (the absorber condition), while the gravitational sector relies on boundary conditions that are assumed rather than derived. The time-symmetry of the linearized Einstein equations is identical to that of Maxwell's equations, but gravity lacks the absorber mechanism that resolves the electromagnetic case.

The "time-symmetry debt" is thus especially acute for gravity: the sector that most naturally *should* admit an absorber condition (massless, long-range, propagating to cosmological distances) is precisely the sector where the absorber mechanism fails due to the extreme weakness of gravitational coupling.
