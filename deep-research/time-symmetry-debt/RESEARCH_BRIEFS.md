## TIME SYMMETRY RESEARCH BRIEFS

### Context for all agents

The Wheeler-Feynman absorber theory establishes that classical electrodynamics is fundamentally time-symmetric. Both retarded and advanced solutions to Maxwell's equations are equally valid. The observed arrow of time in electromagnetic radiation — the fact that we see retarded waves and not advanced ones — is not a property of the dynamics. It is a consequence of a cosmological boundary condition: the universe contains enough future absorbers to produce the observed radiative asymmetry. The absorber condition provides a *structural* justification for the choice of retarded propagator in the U(1) electromagnetic sector.

In quantum field theory, the retarded propagator (or equivalently, the Feynman iε prescription that encodes a specific time-ordering) is chosen by hand in *every* gauge sector — not just electromagnetism but also SU(2) weak and SU(3) strong. The Yang-Mills equations are time-symmetric for any gauge group. The selection of retarded over advanced solutions is a conventional choice that enters silently as an undeclared assumption.

The Wheeler-Feynman absorber mechanism works for electromagnetism because photons are massless and long-range — they propagate to cosmological distances where the absorber condition can operate. The weak and strong sectors do not obviously admit the same mechanism: W and Z bosons are massive (short-range), and gluons are confined. But the time-symmetric structure of the underlying equations is identical in all sectors. The retarded propagator is chosen by hand everywhere. Only one sector has a structural account of why.

This is an open problem. As far as we can determine, nobody in the literature has systematically addressed the time-symmetry debt in the non-abelian gauge sectors. Each of the following research tasks investigates a specific face of this problem.

---

### AGENT 1: The absorber condition in non-abelian gauge theories

**Question:** Has anyone formulated, attempted, or argued for/against an absorber-type condition for non-abelian gauge fields (SU(2) weak or SU(3) strong)? If not, what are the specific mathematical obstacles?

**Research directions:**
- Search for any extension of Wheeler-Feynman or direct-action electrodynamics to non-abelian gauge theories.
- Search for any discussion of the time-symmetric formulation of Yang-Mills theory in the context of the arrow of time, propagator selection, or boundary conditions.
- Investigate whether confinement in QCD and mass generation in the electroweak sector create fundamental obstructions to an absorber-type mechanism, or merely practical ones.
- Look at the direct-action / action-at-a-distance tradition (Schwarzschild, Tetrode, Fokker, Wheeler-Feynman) and determine whether any of these frameworks were ever generalized beyond U(1).
- Investigate the Schwinger-Keldysh (closed time path) formalism, which uses both forward and backward time contours. Does this formalism implicitly encode time-symmetric dynamics in a way relevant to the absorber question? Has anyone interpreted it through that lens?

**Deliverable:** A report on the state of the literature, identifying what exists, what gaps remain, and what the specific technical obstacles are to extending the absorber framework to non-abelian gauge theories.

### AGENT 2: Cosmological selection of the retarded propagator

**Question:** Can the cosmological expansion of the universe provide a universal mechanism for selecting the retarded propagator across all gauge sectors, not just electromagnetism?

**Research directions:**
- There is at least one paper (likely in the MDPI Universe journal, circa 2022-2023) that derives the retarded propagator from the Hubble expansion for quantum fields generally. Find it, read it carefully, and assess its robustness.
- Does this derivation work for interacting fields, or only free fields? Does it survive renormalization?
- Does the derivation apply to massive gauge bosons (W, Z) and confined gauge bosons (gluons), or does it implicitly assume massless propagation?
- Search for other work connecting cosmological boundary conditions (expanding universe, cosmological horizons, de Sitter space) to the selection of propagator type or the arrow of time in QFT generally.
- Investigate the Gold universe hypothesis (time-symmetric cosmology where a collapsing universe would have reversed thermodynamic arrow) and its relation to propagator selection.
- Look at work by Schulman, Price, Penrose (Weyl curvature hypothesis), Carroll, and others on cosmological origins of time asymmetry and assess whether any of them address the propagator choice specifically rather than just thermodynamic entropy.

**Deliverable:** An assessment of whether cosmological expansion can structurally justify the retarded propagator universally, with specific attention to where the argument works, where it breaks down, and what assumptions it requires.

### AGENT 3: CP violation, T violation, and the propagator

**Question:** The weak interaction violates CP symmetry, and by the CPT theorem, this implies T violation. Does this dynamical time-reversal violation in the weak sector have any relationship to the conventional time asymmetry encoded in the propagator choice?

**Research directions:**
- CP violation in the Standard Model enters through the complex phase in the CKM matrix (and possibly the PMNS matrix for neutrinos). This is a *dynamical* source of T violation — actual physical processes that distinguish past from future. The propagator choice is a *kinematic* or *boundary condition* source of time asymmetry. Are these related or completely independent?
- Does CP violation in the weak sector do any of the structural work that the absorber condition does for electromagnetism? I.e., does it provide a mechanism that makes the retarded solution physically distinguished from the advanced solution within the SU(2) sector specifically?
- Search for literature connecting CP violation to the arrow of time. This is a well-known topic (Sakharov conditions for baryogenesis, etc.) but the specific question is narrower: does CP violation bear on the *propagator selection* problem?
- Investigate the strong CP problem and the theta vacuum angle. The strong sector has a *potential* CP-violating parameter (theta) that is observed to be essentially zero. Does the absence of CP violation in QCD have implications for the time-symmetry status of the strong sector?
- Look at work by Sachs, Kabir, and others on T violation in neutral kaon and B meson systems. These are direct experimental observations of T violation. Do any of these authors discuss the relationship between observed T violation and the foundational time asymmetry in the propagator?

**Deliverable:** A clear determination of whether CP/T violation in the weak sector is structurally related to the propagator time-asymmetry problem or whether these are independent issues that happen to share the word "time."

### AGENT 4: Inheritance of time asymmetry through coupling and unification

**Question:** Do the non-abelian gauge sectors inherit their effective time asymmetry from the electromagnetic sector through coupling, and does electroweak unification provide a mechanism for this?

**Research directions:**
- Above the electroweak symmetry-breaking scale (~246 GeV), the electromagnetic U(1) and weak SU(2) are unified into the electroweak SU(2)×U(1) gauge theory. If the U(1) sector has a structural time asymmetry via the absorber condition, does this propagate into the SU(2) sector through the unified electroweak interaction? After symmetry breaking, does the weak sector retain the time asymmetry it inherited from the unified phase?
- All quarks carry electromagnetic charge. The retarded EM propagator constrains quark dynamics through QED vertices. Does this impose an effective time asymmetry on QCD processes involving quarks, even though the gluon sector itself has no absorber condition?
- Search for work on the interplay between different gauge sectors' causal structures. Does the causal structure of one sector constrain the others through shared matter fields?
- Investigate whether the Feynman iε prescription is independently imposed in each gauge sector or whether it is a single global choice that applies uniformly. Is there a consistent formulation of the Standard Model where different gauge sectors have different propagator prescriptions (e.g., retarded for EM, symmetric for QCD)?
- Look at thermal field theory and the KMS (Kubo-Martin-Schwinger) condition. In thermal equilibrium, the propagator structure is constrained by the temperature. Does the thermal state of the early universe impose a uniform propagator choice across all sectors?

**Deliverable:** An assessment of whether time asymmetry in the EM sector can "infect" the other sectors through coupling, and whether electroweak unification provides a specific mechanism for this. Identify whether a mixed propagator prescription (different choices in different sectors) is even mathematically consistent.

### AGENT 5: Gravitational absorber condition

**Question:** Has anyone formulated an absorber condition for gravitational radiation, analogous to the Wheeler-Feynman absorber condition for electromagnetic radiation?

**Research directions:**
- Gravity shares the key features that make the EM absorber condition work: the graviton is massless, gravity is long-range, and the linearized Einstein equations are time-symmetric with both retarded and advanced solutions.
- Search for work on time-symmetric formulations of gravitational radiation. The gravitational wave equation admits advanced solutions just as Maxwell's equations do. Has anyone proposed that gravitational radiation requires an absorber condition for the same reasons EM does?
- Look at the 2025 paper on "Experimental estimation of Asymmetry of Radiation for Wheeler-Feynman theory for gravitational waves" found in the earlier search. What does it propose?
- Investigate Bondi, van der Burg, Metzner (BMS) and the asymptotic structure of gravitational radiation. Does the asymptotic structure of spacetime at null infinity impose any constraint on the retarded vs. advanced character of gravitational waves?
- Search for work by DeWitt, Brehme, Hobbs, and others on gravitational radiation reaction. The gravitational analog of the Abraham-Lorentz force (radiation reaction) has been studied. Is it derived from time-symmetric considerations à la Wheeler-Feynman?
- Look at Penrose's Weyl curvature hypothesis: the Weyl tensor vanishes at the Big Bang (past boundary) but not at the Big Crunch (future boundary). This is an explicitly time-asymmetric cosmological boundary condition on the gravitational field. Does it function as an absorber condition for gravity?
- Consider LIGO/VIRGO observations. All detected gravitational waves are retarded. Has anyone in the gravitational wave community discussed the absence of advanced gravitational waves as a foundational issue rather than simply assuming retarded radiation?

**Deliverable:** A report on whether gravity has its own absorber condition (or equivalent structural mechanism for selecting retarded radiation), with attention to whether Penrose's Weyl curvature hypothesis plays this role. If gravity has a resolved time-symmetry account, the project's structural graph needs to register this alongside EM as a second sector where the debt is discharged.
