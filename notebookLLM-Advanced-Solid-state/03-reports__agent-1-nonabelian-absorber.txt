# Agent 1 Report: The Absorber Condition in Non-Abelian Gauge Theories

## Executive Summary

**No one has formulated an absorber-type condition for non-abelian gauge fields.** The Wheeler-Feynman absorber mechanism -- which provides a structural, cosmological justification for the retarded propagator in U(1) electrodynamics -- has never been extended to SU(2) weak or SU(3) strong gauge theories. The direct-action tradition (Schwarzschild, Tetrode, Fokker, Wheeler-Feynman, Hoyle-Narlikar, Davies, Barut) has been extended only to gravity and to quantum electrodynamics, never to non-abelian gauge sectors. This constitutes a genuine gap in the foundations of the Standard Model: the retarded propagator (encoded as the Feynman iε prescription) is chosen by hand in every gauge sector, but only one sector -- electromagnetism -- has a structural account of why.

The mathematical obstacles to extending the absorber mechanism to non-abelian theories are substantial but not obviously fatal. They fall into three categories: (1) the self-interaction and nonlinearity of non-abelian gauge fields, which obstruct the construction of a Fokker-type direct-action functional; (2) confinement in QCD and mass generation in the electroweak sector, which prevent gauge bosons from propagating to cosmological distances where an absorber condition could operate; and (3) the Gribov ambiguity, which obstructs the construction of unique retarded and advanced Green's functions in non-abelian gauge theory. However, both confinement and electroweak symmetry breaking are temperature-dependent phenomena that disappear at high enough energies, suggesting these obstacles may be epoch-dependent rather than fundamental.

## 1. The Wheeler-Feynman Absorber Theory: What It Achieves

### 1.1 The Classical Theory

Wheeler and Feynman (1945, 1949) showed that classical electrodynamics can be reformulated as a time-symmetric, field-free theory of direct interparticle action. The key elements:

- **The Fokker Action**: The dynamics is derived from a Lorentz-invariant action S describing particle trajectories, consisting of a mass term and an interaction term involving a light-cone delta function constraint. There is no independent field degree of freedom; the electromagnetic field is eliminable in favor of direct particle-particle interactions along the light cone.

- **Time-Symmetric Field**: Each charge emits a field consisting of half-retarded plus half-advanced solutions to Maxwell's equations. This is the natural, time-symmetric solution.

- **The Absorber Condition**: The assumption that the "free field" (the difference between retarded and advanced components) vanishes identically. This requires that the universe act as a perfect absorber -- that all radiation emitted by any charge is eventually completely absorbed by all other charges in the universe.

- **The Result**: If the absorber condition holds, the combined response of all absorbers converts the time-symmetric half-retarded-plus-half-advanced field of any individual emitter into a purely retarded field. The apparent time-asymmetry of radiation is not a property of the dynamical laws but emerges from the cosmological boundary condition of complete future absorption.

**Key references:**
- Wheeler, J.A. & Feynman, R.P. (1945). "Interaction with the Absorber as the Mechanism of Radiation." *Reviews of Modern Physics* 17(2-3): 157-181.
- Wheeler, J.A. & Feynman, R.P. (1949). "Classical Electrodynamics in Terms of Direct Interparticle Action." *Reviews of Modern Physics* 21(3): 425-433.

### 1.2 Quantum Extensions

- **Davies (1971, 1972)** developed a quantum relativistic extension of the Wheeler-Feynman theory using the S-matrix technique, treating source particles as indistinguishable fermions with antiparticles, and incorporating the absorber response without allusion to specific absorber mechanisms.
  - Davies, P.C.W. (1971). "Extension of Wheeler-Feynman Quantum Theory to the Relativistic Domain. I. Scattering Processes." *J. Phys. A: Gen. Phys.* 6, 836.
  - Davies, P.C.W. (1972). "Extension of Wheeler-Feynman Quantum Theory to the Relativistic Domain. II. Emission Processes." *J. Phys. A: Gen. Phys.* 5, 1025-1036.

- **Hoyle and Narlikar (1995)** reviewed the full development of action-at-a-distance electrodynamics at both classical and quantum levels, including generalization to curved spacetime. Their quantum generalization of the Fokker action claims to be free of the divergence problems that trouble both classical and quantum field theory. They state that "all the results of physics can be obtained from interparticle action -- for decay problems and scattering problems alike."
  - Hoyle, F. & Narlikar, J.V. (1995). "Cosmology and action-at-a-distance electrodynamics." *Reviews of Modern Physics* 67(1): 113-155.

- **Pegg (1975)** surveyed developments in quantum absorber theory, showing that field quantization can be replaced by the response of the absorber, and that this approach can reproduce spontaneous emission, the Lamb shift, the dynamic Stark effect, and photoelectron antibunching -- effects often attributed to the quantized field.
  - Pegg, D.T. (1975). "Absorber Theory of Radiation." *Reports on Progress in Physics* 38(12): 1339-1383.

- **Barut (1983-1998)** developed "self-field QED," a direct-action formulation that eliminates local electromagnetic field variables entirely, treating all physical effects as arising from matter interacting directly with matter. His program produced 42+ papers reproducing QED results without field quantization, but he did not extend the framework beyond electromagnetism.

- **Kastner (2015)** showed that one can dispense with the Feynman propagator in a direct-action theory of fields while retaining the interpretation of negative energy solutions as antiparticles, demonstrating that the direct-action framework is compatible with modern relativistic quantum mechanics.
  - Kastner, R.E. (2015). "Antimatter in the direct-action theory of fields." arXiv:1509.06040.

### 1.3 Gravitational Extension

Hoyle and Narlikar also proposed an absorber-based theory of gravity in the context of general relativity, inspired by the Machian nature of the Wheeler-Feynman framework. Sciama (1953) independently explored a gravitational absorber mechanism in connection with Mach's principle, postulating that inertial forces arise from a gravity-based radiative field created by distant cosmic matter propagating both forward and backward in time. Narlikar's 2003 review article covers these developments comprehensively.

- Narlikar, J.V. (2003). "Action at a Distance and Cosmology: A Historical Perspective." *Annual Review of Astronomy and Astrophysics* 41: 169-189.

### 1.4 The Cramer Transactional Interpretation

Cramer (1986) generalized the Wheeler-Feynman paradigm to quantum mechanics as a whole: emission is a time-symmetric "handshake" between quantum offer and confirmation waves mediated by absorber responses. However, the transactional interpretation operates at the level of quantum mechanics, not quantum field theory, and does not address the specific question of gauge-sector-dependent absorber conditions.

- Cramer, J.G. (1986). "The Transactional Interpretation of Quantum Mechanics." *Reviews of Modern Physics* 58(3): 647-688.

## 2. The Gap: No Absorber Condition for Non-Abelian Gauge Fields

### 2.1 What the Literature Does Not Contain

After extensive search of the academic literature, I find **no paper, preprint, or book** that:

1. Formulates an absorber-type condition for SU(2) or SU(3) gauge fields.
2. Constructs a Fokker-type direct-action functional for non-abelian gauge theory.
3. Develops a Wheeler-Feynman-style time-symmetric formulation of Yang-Mills theory.
4. Investigates the physical or cosmological basis for choosing the retarded propagator specifically in the weak or strong sectors.
5. Discusses whether confinement or electroweak symmetry breaking constitute fundamental or merely practical obstacles to a non-abelian absorber mechanism.

The direct-action tradition (Schwarzschild-Tetrode-Fokker-Wheeler-Feynman-Hoyle-Narlikar-Davies-Pegg-Barut-Kastner) has produced a rich body of work on U(1) electrodynamics and gravitational theory. It has **never been extended to non-abelian gauge theories.** Narlikar's 2003 review -- the most comprehensive historical survey of action-at-a-distance physics -- covers only electromagnetism and gravity; the strong and weak interactions are not mentioned.

Hoyle and Narlikar's 1974 book *Action at a Distance in Physics and Cosmology* (Freeman, 266 pp.) covers: classical theories of electromagnetism and gravitation, the Wheeler-Feynman absorber theory, the quantum response of the universe, direct-particle theories in Riemannian spacetime, inertia and gravitation. The strong and weak nuclear forces are not discussed.

### 2.2 The Universal iε Prescription

The gap is particularly striking because the iε prescription -- which encodes the choice of retarded propagator in the Feynman rules -- is applied identically across all gauge sectors of the Standard Model. As Donoghue and Menezes (2019, 2020) have emphasized:

- "Hidden in our conventions for quantization is a connection to the definition of an arrow of causality" -- the sign of iε determines which time direction is "past" and which is "future."
- If quantization conventions are mixed within the same theory (different signs of iε for different fields), microcausality is violated.
- The choice of ±iε is not driven by a desire to impose a preferred direction of time or causality -- both versions are equally causal, with different directions of causality.
- Under time-reversal, the Feynman propagator Z+ is turned into Z-, reversing the arrow of causality.

**Crucially, Donoghue and Menezes do not discuss the Wheeler-Feynman absorber theory, and they do not address why the same sign convention must be used across all gauge sectors. They treat the sign choice as conventional rather than physically justified.** Their paper operates at a foundational level using generic scalar field examples; Standard Model particles (gluons, W/Z bosons) are not specifically discussed.

- Donoghue, J.F. & Menezes, G. (2019). "Arrow of Causality and Quantum Gravity." *Physical Review Letters* 123: 171601.
- Donoghue, J.F. & Menezes, G. (2020). "Quantum causality and the arrows of time and thermodynamics." *Progress in Particle and Nuclear Physics* 115: 103812.

The situation is therefore: the retarded propagator choice is recognized as conventional (not derived from dynamics), its universality across gauge sectors is recognized as necessary for consistency, but no physical mechanism -- analogous to the absorber condition in electrodynamics -- is invoked to justify it in any sector other than U(1).

## 3. Mathematical Obstacles to a Non-Abelian Absorber Condition

### 3.1 Obstacle 1: Self-Interaction and Nonlinearity

The most fundamental mathematical obstacle is the self-interaction of non-abelian gauge fields. In electromagnetism, photons do not carry electric charge and do not interact with each other. This linearity is essential to the Wheeler-Feynman construction:

- The electromagnetic field can be decomposed into retarded and advanced components that superpose linearly.
- The field of multiple charges is the sum of individual single-charge fields.
- The Fokker action is bilinear in the charges -- it describes pairwise interactions without field self-interaction.

In non-abelian gauge theory, the gauge bosons carry color charge (gluons) or weak isospin (W bosons) and interact with themselves. The field strength tensor gains nonlinear terms:

F_μν^a = ∂_μ A_ν^a - ∂_ν A_μ^a + g f^{abc} A_μ^b A_ν^c

This has several consequences:

(a) **No superposition principle.** The Yang-Mills equations are nonlinear, so the retarded field of a multi-particle system is not simply the sum of individual retarded fields. Kosyakov (1994, 1998) found exact retarded solutions for classical SU(N) Yang-Mills equations with colored point-particle sources and showed that, remarkably, the retarded non-abelian solutions for multiple quarks can be written as a sum of single-quark terms -- but this is a property of the specific retarded solution, not of the equations in general. The superposition is exact for retarded solutions but there is no reason to expect it extends to arbitrary linear combinations of retarded and advanced solutions.
  - Kosyakov, B.P. (1994). "Exact solutions of the Yang-Mills equations with source in the form of two point color charges." *Theoretical and Mathematical Physics*.
  - Kosyakov, B.P. (1998). "Exact solutions in the Yang-Mills-Wong theory." *Physical Review D* 57: 5032. arXiv:hep-th/9902039.

(b) **No obvious Fokker-type action.** The Fokker action in electrodynamics eliminates the field entirely, leaving only particle-particle interactions. In non-abelian gauge theory, the field has independent dynamical content because of self-interaction. Gluon-gluon vertices (both triple and quartic) represent field degrees of freedom that cannot be eliminated in favor of particle-particle interactions. A Fokker action is, by construction, a functional of particle worldlines alone. The self-interacting gluon field cannot be reduced to worldline data.

(c) **Gauge field carries charge.** In the Fokker construction, the mediating field is neutral -- it couples to charges but carries no charge itself. Gluons carry color charge and W bosons carry weak isospin. This means the "absorber" for gluon radiation would need to include the gluon field itself as both emitter and absorber, creating a circularity that has no analog in the electromagnetic case.

### 3.2 Obstacle 2: Confinement (QCD) and Mass Generation (Electroweak)

The Wheeler-Feynman absorber condition requires that radiation propagate to cosmological distances where the collective response of all future absorbers can produce the observed retarded radiation pattern. This works for photons because they are massless and long-range.

**For gluons (SU(3) strong):**
- Color confinement prevents gluons from propagating freely beyond hadronic scales (~1 fm = 10^{-15} m). The strong force between quarks does not diminish with distance; instead, the gluon flux tube between separating color charges stores increasing energy until a new quark-antiquark pair is produced.
- The gluon propagator in the infrared is massive: lattice simulations show a dynamical mass generation for gluons in the low-energy regime. The gluon propagator violates reflection positivity, meaning gluons cannot be interpreted as particles in the physical spectrum -- they are confined.
- There is no cosmological-scale gluon radiation that could be absorbed by distant matter. The absorber condition, which requires cosmological completeness of absorption, has no obvious arena in which to operate.

**For W and Z bosons (SU(2) weak):**
- The W boson (mass ~80.4 GeV) and Z boson (mass ~91.2 GeV) are massive. Their propagators fall off exponentially at distances beyond ~10^{-18} m (the Yukawa range). They decay rapidly (lifetime ~3 x 10^{-25} s).
- Massive gauge bosons cannot propagate to cosmological distances. The absorber condition requires infinite-range propagation; massive propagation is exponentially suppressed.

**However, these obstacles are temperature-dependent:**
- Above the QCD deconfinement temperature (~155-175 MeV, or ~2 x 10^{12} K), quarks and gluons are deconfined in a quark-gluon plasma. Gluons can propagate freely over distances much larger than hadronic scales. The confinement obstacle disappears.
- Above the electroweak symmetry restoration temperature (~160 GeV, or ~2 x 10^{15} K, occurring ~10^{-12} s after the Big Bang), the Higgs vacuum expectation value vanishes, the W and Z bosons become massless, and the electroweak gauge symmetry is unbroken. The mass obstacle disappears.

This means that in the early universe, all gauge bosons of the Standard Model were massless and long-range. The question then becomes: **could an absorber condition have been established in the early universe, when all sectors were symmetric and long-range, and could this condition have persisted through the phase transitions that introduced confinement and mass?** This question has never been posed in the literature.

### 3.3 Obstacle 3: The Gribov Ambiguity

A subtler technical obstacle concerns the definition of retarded and advanced Green's functions in non-abelian gauge theory.

In electrodynamics, the retarded Green's function is unique and well-defined. In non-abelian gauge theory, the gauge-fixing procedure is complicated by Gribov copies -- distinct gauge field configurations that satisfy the same gauge-fixing condition but are related by "large" gauge transformations. No unique and continuous gauge-fixing condition exists for non-abelian gauge theories (the Gribov problem), and the number of Gribov copies grows exponentially with the volume.

As noted in the search results: "The classical equations of motion for gauge fields are not deterministic -- they do not determine the gauge -- so one does not have unique advanced and retarded propagators." This is a fundamental difficulty for any program that seeks to construct a direct-action theory based on retarded and advanced Green's functions.

The Gribov problem is purely a non-abelian phenomenon. It does not arise in electrodynamics. It is "completely uncritical classically" but becomes problematic upon quantization. This means the very mathematical objects (unique retarded and advanced propagators) that the Wheeler-Feynman construction relies upon may not exist in non-abelian gauge theory in a gauge-invariant way.

### 3.4 Obstacle 4: The Topology of the Non-Abelian Vacuum

The non-abelian vacuum has a nontrivial topological structure (instantons, θ-vacua) that has no analog in electrodynamics. The θ parameter introduces CP violation and breaks time-reversal symmetry at θ ≠ 0 and θ ≠ π. SU(N) gauge theory is time-reversal invariant only at θ = 0 and θ = π. At θ = π, there is a discrete 't Hooft anomaly involving time reversal and center symmetry.

This topological structure means that time-reversal symmetry in non-abelian gauge theory is more subtle than in electrodynamics. The absorber construction requires the fundamental dynamics to be time-symmetric; in non-abelian theories, this is true only for specific values of the vacuum angle.

## 4. The Schwinger-Keldysh Formalism: A Partial Bridge?

The Schwinger-Keldysh (SK) or closed-time-path (CTP) formalism, developed by Schwinger (1961) and Keldysh (1964), is the closest existing framework to a time-symmetric treatment of non-abelian gauge theories in QFT. Key features:

- **Doubled time contour.** The SK formalism doubles the field variables to capture forward and backward time evolution along a closed contour in the complex time plane. This structure inherently handles both time directions.

- **Full set of propagators.** The formalism naturally yields retarded, advanced, Keldysh (symmetric), and Wightman correlation functions -- not just the Feynman propagator.

- **Application to non-abelian theories.** The SK formalism has been applied to SU(N) gauge theories, including lattice formulations. Recent work (2023) has achieved stable simulations of SU(2) Yang-Mills theory on time contours whose real-time extent exceeds the inverse temperature, using time-reflection-symmetric formulations with anisotropic kernels.

- **Causality enforcement.** Despite using both forward and backward contours, the SK formalism enforces causality through retarded boundary conditions: "amplitudes are manifestly causal, meaning the source and detector are always linked by a connected chain of retarded propagators."

**However, the SK formalism has never been interpreted through the lens of the absorber question.** No one has asked whether the backward time contour in SK could be understood as encoding the response of future absorbers, or whether the emergence of retarded propagators from the doubled-contour structure has any connection to a cosmological absorber condition. The formalism is used as a computational tool for non-equilibrium dynamics, not as a foundational framework for understanding time-asymmetry.

The parallel is suggestive: the SK formalism contains both forward and backward time evolution (analogous to retarded and advanced solutions), it applies to non-abelian gauge theories, and it produces retarded propagators as observable quantities. Whether this constitutes an implicit absorber mechanism, or merely a mathematical convenience, is an open question that has not been explored.

## 5. Classical Yang-Mills Solutions and the Retarded/Advanced Distinction

### 5.1 Kosyakov's Exact Retarded Solutions

B.P. Kosyakov has produced the most detailed study of classical retarded solutions in non-abelian gauge theory. His work on the Yang-Mills-Wong theory (Yang-Mills fields coupled to classical colored point particles via the Wong equations) yields:

- Exact retarded solutions for SU(N) Yang-Mills equations with multiple colored point-particle sources.
- A surprising result: retarded solutions for multiple quarks can be written as the sum of single-quark terms, despite the nonlinearity.
- Stable solutions suitable for modeling a nonperturbative gluon vacuum.
- A proof that confinement cannot be described within classical Yang-Mills theory.

Kosyakov's textbook *Introduction to the Classical Theory of Particles and Fields* (Springer, 2007) provides the most comprehensive treatment of classical non-abelian field theory with point sources. He works exclusively with retarded solutions and does not consider advanced solutions or time-symmetric formulations.

### 5.2 The Absence of Advanced Solutions

No paper in the literature constructs exact advanced solutions for the Yang-Mills equations with colored sources, or studies the time-symmetric combination (half-retarded + half-advanced) for non-abelian fields. This is the foundational step that Wheeler and Feynman took for electrodynamics in the 1940s, and it has never been attempted for Yang-Mills theory.

## 7. Assessment: What Remains Open

### 7.1 The Core Gap

The retarded propagator (Feynman iε prescription) is chosen by hand in every gauge sector of the Standard Model. Only the U(1) electromagnetic sector has a structural account (the Wheeler-Feynman absorber condition) for why the retarded solution is selected over the advanced one. The SU(2) and SU(3) sectors have no such account.

### 7.2 Whether the Gap Is Merely Practical or Fundamental

The obstacles to extending the absorber mechanism to non-abelian sectors are real but may not be fundamental:

**Arguments that the obstacles are fundamental:**
- Gluon self-interaction makes a Fokker-type action impossible to construct (the field has independent degrees of freedom that cannot be eliminated).
- The Gribov ambiguity may prevent the construction of unique retarded/advanced propagators in a gauge-invariant way.
- Confinement and mass generation prevent gauge bosons from reaching cosmological absorbers.

**Arguments that the obstacles are merely practical:**
- At temperatures above the QCD deconfinement transition (~10^{12} K) and the electroweak symmetry restoration (~10^{15} K), all gauge bosons are massless and long-range. In the early universe, the obstacles vanish.
- The Gribov problem is perturbatively harmless and may be manageable in a suitably formulated theory.
- The Schwinger-Keldysh formalism already treats non-abelian gauge theories with both time directions and produces retarded propagators, suggesting the mathematical structure exists even if it has not been interpreted in absorber terms.
- Asymptotic freedom means that at short distances (high energies), gluons behave as nearly free, weakly-coupled massless particles -- exactly the regime where an absorber-type mechanism could operate.

### 7.3 Unexplored Directions

Several research directions have never been pursued:

1. **Classical time-symmetric Yang-Mills solutions.** No one has constructed the half-retarded + half-advanced solution for the Yang-Mills equations with sources and studied its properties. This is the non-abelian analog of the first step in the Wheeler-Feynman program.

2. **Absorber condition in the early universe.** Before confinement and electroweak symmetry breaking, all gauge fields were massless and long-range. Could an absorber condition have been established during this epoch? Could the subsequent phase transitions preserve the retarded character of radiation established during the symmetric phase?

3. **SK formalism as implicit absorber theory.** The Schwinger-Keldysh formalism, with its doubled time contour and natural production of retarded propagators for non-abelian gauge theories, has never been interpreted through the absorber lens.

4. **Direct-action functional for non-abelian fields.** While a pure Fokker action (eliminating all field degrees of freedom) appears impossible for self-interacting gauge fields, it may be possible to construct a *partial* direct-action formulation that eliminates the matter-mediated components while retaining the self-interaction sector -- a hybrid that preserves some features of the absorber framework.

5. **Topological obstructions.** The relationship between the θ-vacuum structure, time-reversal symmetry at specific θ values, and the absorber condition has never been investigated.

---

## 8. Conclusion

The question "Has anyone formulated an absorber-type condition for non-abelian gauge fields?" has a clear answer: **No.** The entire direct-action/absorber tradition in physics has remained confined to U(1) electrodynamics and gravity. The non-abelian sectors of the Standard Model inherit their causal structure (the retarded propagator) from a universal convention -- the sign of the iε prescription -- that is applied identically across all sectors but justified in none beyond electromagnetism.

The mathematical obstacles are real: self-interaction and nonlinearity of non-abelian fields obstruct the Fokker construction; confinement and mass generation prevent long-range propagation of gauge bosons; the Gribov ambiguity complicates the definition of retarded/advanced propagators; and the topological structure of the non-abelian vacuum introduces subtleties in time-reversal symmetry. But these obstacles are regime-dependent. In the early universe, above the electroweak and QCD phase transitions, all gauge bosons were massless and free-propagating, and the obstacles did not exist. The question of whether an absorber condition could operate in this symmetric phase, and whether its consequences could persist through the subsequent symmetry-breaking transitions, has never been asked.

This represents a genuine lacuna in the foundations of quantum field theory: a structural asymmetry in the theoretical justification for a choice that is operationally identical across all sectors of the Standard Model.
